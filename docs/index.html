How it works: The engine audio system reads throttle input and dynamically adjusts the playback rate (pitch) and gain (volume) of a pre-recorded engine sound loop. At idle (0%), the engine runs slowly and quietly. As throttle increases, pitch and volume scale up smoothly. Rev transients (sudden throttle spikes) apply a 25% boost to pitch and 40% boost to volume for realistic engine response.
